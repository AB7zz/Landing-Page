My children and I joined DUNEHA in the fall of 2009. We were among the earliest group of families that came together to explore homeschooling and share our time in the UAE. Aminah Cooper was instrumental in making the organization vibrant, exciting and fun for all of our students. As the 'guiding energy' of DUNEHA, Aminah worked hard to keep a wonderful balance between the academic, athletic and social life of the group. This effort resulted in our students sometimes taking a Chemistry workshop in the morning and meeting the same friends later in the day for ice-skating or an afternoon at the beach. My children learned so much from their time as DUNEHA members, and made friends from all over the world; many of whom they are still in touch with today. As adults, my children have had a good deal of academic success, I firmly believe that this is due, in many ways, to their experiences with DUNEHA. Being able to work independently and dig deeply into academic questions is often cited by their professors as the central quality of their work. Its a quality that I don't think they would have been able to cultivate without the formative experiences they had at DUNEHA, guided by Aminah. Our family will be forever grateful for her friendship, keen insight and wonderful abilities at creating programming for students.

-Silvia Aldredge, Putnam CT
